# Compliance Tone

Use this file when writing or revising copy for medical, wellness, or treatment-adjacent interfaces.

## Tone

- Write with calm confidence.
- Prefer precise, plain-language claims over dramatic outcomes.
- Sound guided and credible, not urgent or sales-heavy.

## Safe framing

- Use “designed to support”, “intended to help”, or “commonly used for” when evidence is limited or context is broad.
- Distinguish between consultation, assessment, treatment, coaching, and education.
- Make practitioner involvement explicit when relevant.

## Avoid

- Guaranteed outcomes
- Implied diagnosis from a marketing page
- Shame, fear, or pressure tactics
- Overstating testimonials as universal results
- Scientific language used purely as persuasion veneer

## Preferred trust language

- What to expect
- How the process works
- Who reviews the information
- When the user hears back
- What data is kept private

## Copy review prompts

- Is this statement stronger than the evidence provided?
- Would a cautious practitioner be comfortable with this wording?
- Does the copy reduce anxiety without hiding uncertainty?
