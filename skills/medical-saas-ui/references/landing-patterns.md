# Landing Patterns

Use this file for marketing surfaces: homepage, program page, pricing, assessment funnel, booking flow, and treatment information pages.

## Recommended page order

1. Hero with one high-trust CTA
2. Proof block
3. Process or protocol steps
4. Who it is for
5. Expected experience or timeline
6. Results framing or testimonials
7. Pricing or next-step clarity
8. FAQ
9. Final reassurance CTA

## Hero rules

- Start with the practical outcome, not abstract inspiration.
- Pair the headline with a trust cue: practitioner, methodology, review count, timeframe, or privacy.
- Use one dominant CTA and one lower-emphasis secondary action at most.
- Keep the above-the-fold area calm. Avoid overcrowding with badges and metrics.

## Trust blocks

Include 2 to 4 of these, integrated near the top rather than buried:

- Named practitioner or clinical team
- Methodology summary
- Privacy or discretion statement
- Testimonials with context
- Press, certification, or partner marks when legitimate
- Transparent expectations about timing and commitment

## Pricing and offer framing

- Explain what is included before showing the price.
- Frame payment as part of a guided plan, not as a hard sell.
- Use comparison tables only when the distinctions are genuinely meaningful.
- If price sensitivity is likely, offer a lower-friction next step such as assessment or consultation.

## Quiz and assessment flows

- Keep the initial screen low pressure: purpose, time required, and what happens after.
- Ask easy questions first; reserve sensitive inputs for later.
- Show visible progress and preserve momentum with short steps.
- Use reassuring microcopy around health data and privacy.

## Before/after and testimonial framing

- Prefer “experience” and “reported outcomes” language when certainty is limited.
- Add context: time period, adherence, practitioner involvement, or sample size when available.
- Avoid sensational contrast, over-editing, or miracle transformation cues.

## CTA examples by intent

- Book a consultation
- Start your assessment
- See if the program fits
- Continue your care plan
- Review treatment options

## Mobile notes

- Keep the CTA visible early without sticky clutter.
- Collapse proof and FAQ cleanly.
- Use stacked cards and single-column sections.
- Do not force comparison tables or wide testimonial grids on mobile.
